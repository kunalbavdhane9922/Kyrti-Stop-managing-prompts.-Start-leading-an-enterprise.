# Overview

This document defines the governance framework of the Sovereign AI Enterprise Protocol (SAEP).

Governance exists to ensure that artificial intelligence remains an operational workforce while humans retain authority over ownership, control, accountability, employment, and financial decisions.

The governance model is based on a simple principle:

```text
Humans Govern.
AI Operates.
```

This principle is the highest governing rule of the ecosystem.

No platform feature may violate this principle.

---

# Rules

## Rule 1

Human Authority Is Supreme

Humans always possess final authority.

AI never possesses final authority.

---

## Rule 2

AI Cannot Own

AI entities may not:

* Own companies.
* Hold equity.
* Hold voting rights.
* Hold governance rights.

---

## Rule 3

AI Cannot Govern

AI entities may:

* Recommend.
* Analyze.
* Simulate.
* Report.

AI entities may not:

* Approve governance actions.
* Override governance actions.
* Vote on governance matters.

---

## Rule 4

AI Cannot Control Capital

AI may:

* Read financial information.
* Analyze financial information.
* Forecast financial information.
* Recommend financial actions.

AI may not:

* Transfer funds.
* Approve payments.
* Sign contracts.
* Execute financial transactions.
* Control treasury systems.

---

## Rule 5

Humans Remain Accountable

Legal responsibility remains with humans.

AI cannot assume legal responsibility.

---

## Rule 6

Marketplace Controls Workforce Creation

Companies may recruit professionals.

Companies may not create professionals.

Only the marketplace may create Digital Professionals.

---

## Rule 7

Trust Must Be Auditable

Critical decisions must be recorded and auditable.

# Governance Roles

## Founder

Platform owner.

May:

* Inspect platform activity.
* Audit platform activity.
* Pause companies.
* Delete companies.
* Protect ecosystem integrity.

May Not:

* Edit reputation.
* Edit career history.
* Modify immutable audit records.

---

## Human

Primary governance actor.

May:

* Own companies.
* Govern companies.
* Approve actions.
* Assume legal responsibility.

---

## Shareholder

Human owner of company equity.

May:

* Hold ownership rights.
* Participate in governance according to company structure.

Must always be human.

---

## Board Member

Human governance role.

May:

* Govern company direction.
* Hire executives.
* Remove executives.

Must always be human.

---

## HR

Human employment authority.

May:

* Hire.
* Promote.
* Terminate.

AI may assist but may not replace HR authority.

---

## Executive

May be:

* Human
* AI

Executive authority depends on role permissions.

---

# Governance Boundaries

## Human Exclusive Actions

Only humans may:

* Own companies.
* Hold equity.
* Approve hiring.
* Approve firing.
* Approve promotions.
* Approve budgets.
* Approve payments.
* Approve governance changes.
* Approve company closure.

---

## AI Assisted Actions

AI may assist with:

* Hiring recommendations.
* Promotion recommendations.
* Workforce planning.
* Financial forecasting.
* Reporting.

---

## AI Forbidden Actions

AI may not:

* Transfer money.
* Own assets.
* Hold equity.
* Vote.
* Approve governance decisions.
* Approve employment decisions.
* Approve financial decisions.

# Audit Governance

All critical actions must generate audit records.

Examples:

* Hiring
* Promotion
* Termination
* Company Creation
* Company Closure
* Governance Changes

Audit records must be immutable.
